Scope and Limitations
This study focuses on the design and development of a Progressive Web-Based Human Resource Information System (HRIS) for the Schools Division Office of the Department of Education in Lucena City. The system aims to centralize personnel records, integrate attendance data, automate leave management processes, and generate reports related to payroll, attendance, and employee information.
The system will include modules for employee information management, attendance monitoring, leave processing, payroll reporting, applicant tracking, and performance evaluation through the digital Individual Performance Commitment and Review Form (IPCRF). Additionally, the system will provide descriptive analytics dashboards that present workforce information using charts and graphical reports.
The proposed system includes a Progressive Web Application (PWA) that allows employees to access HR services via mobile devices, such as viewing attendance records, checking pay slips, and filing leave applications. It also implements security features like Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to limit access based on user roles, along with audit logs to track system activities and ensure data integrity and accountability.
However, this study has several limitations. The system will be implemented specifically for the Schools Division Office of Lucena City and may not immediately cover other divisions of the Department of Education. Furthermore, the system will focus on descriptive analytics, which summarizes and visualizes existing data, and will not include advanced predictive analytics or artificial intelligence features.
